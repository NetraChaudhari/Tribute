Biography

Lata Mangeshkar is a legendary Indian playback singer and one of the most iconic voices in the history of Indian music. Born on September 28, 1929, in Indore, Madhya Pradesh, she belongs to a musical family. Her father, Pandit Deenanath Mangeshkar, was a classical singer and theater actor, and her mother, Shevanti, was also a trained classical singer. Lata Mangeshkar began her music training at a young age, and her father was her first guru.

Lata Mangeshkar started her career in the 1940s and went on to sing for over 70 years, recording more than 30,000 songs in various Indian languages, including Hindi, Marathi, Bengali, Gujarati, and Punjabi. She has worked with several legendary composers, including S.D. Burman, R.D. Burman, Naushad, Shankar Jaikishan, and A.R. Rahman. Her voice has been described as soulful, melodious, and versatile, and she is known for her ability to convey emotions through her singing.

Some of her most popular songs include "Aayega Aanewala" from the film Mahal (1949), "Pyar Kiya To Darna Kya" from Mughal-e-Azam (1960), "Lag Ja Gale" from Woh Kaun Thi (1964), and "Tere Bina Zindagi Se" from Aandhi (1975). She has received numerous awards and honors for her contribution to Indian music, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, and the Dadasaheb Phalke Award, India's highest award for contribution to Indian cinema.

Apart from singing, Lata Mangeshkar has also acted in a few films and composed music for some Marathi films. She has also been actively involved in various charitable and social causes, including supporting the education of underprivileged children and promoting organ donation.

Today, Lata Mangeshkar is considered a national treasure in India and an icon in the world of music. Her contribution to Indian music has been immense, and her songs continue to be loved by music lovers around the world.
